About SCDFinder

SCDFinder is used to search for SCD domains, comprised of multiple S/T-Q motifs clustered in a stretch of amino acids. SCD domains are known to be present in proteins that are phosphorylated by ATM and ATR, two master regulators of the DNA Damage Response (DDR).

As a result of different factors DNA can suffer a variety of injuries, which must be repaired in order to avoid genome instability, and prevent tumorigenesis. ATM and ATR, coordinate a response to DNA damage that includes halting of the cell cycle, enzymatic repair of the DNA injury and triggering of programmed cell death when necessary. Previous studies have shown that ATM and ATR targets usually contain an SCD domain, which was originally defined as the presence of 3 motifs of either serine or threonine, followed by a glutamine anywhere within a stretch of 100 amino acids. The variable nature of this definition makes this particular domain difficult to predict using traditional motif and domain finding tools.

SCDFinder scans and analyzes a protein, or all the proteins within a specific organism's proteome for the presence of SCDs. It allows the user to stipulate the SCD definition and helps identify putative ATM/ATR targets. SCDFinder is the product of an undergraduate research project by students and faculty from the Department of Biology and the Department of Mathematics, Computer Science and Cooperative Engineering at the University of St. Thomas (UST) in Houston, Texas.
